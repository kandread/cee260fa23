Sample mean

If \(n\) observations in a sample are \(x_1, x_2, ... , x_n\) the sample mean is \[\overline{x} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \dfrac{x_i}{n}\]

Sample mean is a weighted average

Assume a variable \(x_i\) with \(k\) distinct values, each with a frequency of \(f_i\)

Sample mean of a regression

Assume that for constants \(a\) and \(b\) we have \[y_i = a x_i + b\]

Example

Suppose we are designing a nylon connector for an auto engine application, with the design wall thickness being 3/32 inches which can affect the connector's pull-off force. If that force is too low, the connector may fail. Eight prototype units were produced with measurements of the pull-off force (in pounds) being 12.6, 12.9, 13.4, 12.3, 13.6, 13.5, 12.6, and 13.1. Calculate the sample mean.

Sample median

Order the \(n\) values from smallest to largest. For odd \(n\), the median is the value in position \((n+1)/2\), otherwise it is the average of the values at positions \(n/2\) and \(n/2+1\).

Sample mode

The value that occurs with the greatest frequency.

Sample variance

If \(n\) observations in a sample are \(x_1, x_2, ... , x_n\) the sample variance is \[s^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \dfrac{(x_i - \overline{x})^2}{(n-1)}\]

Sample standard deviation

The standard deviation is the square root of variance \[s = \sqrt{s^2} = \sqrt{ \sum_{i=1}^{n} \dfrac{(x_i - \overline{x})^2}{n-1}}\]

Sample percentiles

The sample \(100p\) percentile of a dataset is that value such that \(100p\) percent of the data values are less than or equal to it, \(0 \leq p \leq 1\).

  • At least \(np\) of the values are less than or equal to it
  • At least \(n(1-p)\) of the values are greater than or equal to it
  • Each 25th percentile is called the quartile

Empirical rule

  1. Approximately 68 percent of the observations lie within \(\overline{x} \pm s\)
  2. Approximately 95 percent of the observations lie within \(\overline{x} \pm 2s\)
  3. Approximately 99.7 percent of the observations lie within \(\overline{x} \pm 3s\)

Correlation

If \((x_i, y_i)\) \(i=1,2,...,n\) are data pairs and \(s_x\), \(s_y\) are the sample standard deviations, the sample correlation coefficient is given by \[r=\dfrac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \overline{x})(y_i - \overline{y})}{(n-1)s_x s_y}\]

Some notes

  • \(-1 \leq r \leq 1\)
  • Correlation only measures linear relationship between two variables!
  • Implications for causality
  • Always plot the data and check for outliers
