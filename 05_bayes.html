Recap on conditional probability

  • When prior information is available
  • Probability of \(A\) given \(B\)

Conditional probability of an event A given event B (\(P(B)>0\)) is denoted as \(P(A|B)\) and calculated from \[P(A|B) = \dfrac{P(AB)}{P(B)}\]

  • \(P(A)\) is the unconditional probability
  • Statistically independent events \(P(A|B)=P(A)\) and \(P(B|A)=P(B)\)

If \(A\) and \(B\) are statistically independent then \[P(A \cap B)=P(A) P(B)\]

Example

  • A lab test is 99% effective in detecting a disease when it is present.
  • It also yields a false positive for 1% of healthy people tested.
  • If 0.5% of the population has the disease, what is the probability that a person who tested positive actually has the disease?
  • Since 0.5% have the disease, 1 out of 200 tested will have it
  • On average, for every 200 people the test will correctly confirm that (0.99)(1) have the disease
  • On average, the test will also incorrectly state that (0.01)(199) people have the disease
  • The proportion of people having the disease out of all the positive tests is
  • \(\dfrac{(0.99)(1)}{(0.99)(1)+(0.01)(199)}=\)
  • \(0.3322\)

Let D be the event that the tested person has the disease and E the event that his test result is positive

\[P(D|E)=\dfrac{P(DE)}{P(E)}\]

\[=\dfrac{P(E|D)P(D)}{P(E|D)P(D)+P(E|D^c)P(D^c)}\]

\[=\dfrac{(0.99)(0.005)}{(0.99)(0.005)+(0.01)(0.995)}=0.3322\]

Bayes theorem

  • Statistical inference
  • Frequentist vs Bayesian
  • Example of the color for 24 circles
  • "If you pick a random circle, 75% chance that it is blue although it could be 74% or 76%"
  • "I am 99% confident that there are 18 blue circles"

But what is the equation?

\[P(A|B) = \dfrac{P(B|A) \times P(A)}{P(B)}\]

\(P(B): \textbf{evidence}\), \(P(A|B): \textbf{posterior}\), \(P(A): \textbf{prior}\), \(P(B|A): \textbf{likelihood}\)

\[P(A|B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \Rightarrow P(A \cap B) = P(A|B) P(B)\]

\[P(B|A) = \dfrac{P(B \cap A)}{P(A)} \Rightarrow P(A \cap B) = P(B|A) P(A)\]

\[P(A|B)P(B) = P(B|A)P(A) \Rightarrow P(A|B)=\dfrac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}\]

A more general equation

If \(E_1,E_2,...,E_k\) are mutually exclusive and exhaustive events and \(B\) is any event with \(PB)>0\) \[P(E_1|B) = \dfrac{P(B|E_1)P(E_1)}{P(B|E_1)P(E_1)+...+P(B|E_k)P(E_k)}\]
