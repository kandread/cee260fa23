Total probability theorem (multiple events)

Assume \(E_1\), \(E_2\),…,\(E_k\) are mutually exclusive and exhaustive sets. Then

\[P(B) = P(B \cap E_1) + P(B \cap E_2) + ... + P(B \cap E_k)\] \[P(B) = P(B|E_1)P(E_1)+P(B|E_2)P(E_2)+ ... + P(B|E_k)P(E_k)\]

Problem 6.1

Highways I1 and I2 merge into I3: I1 and I2 have the same traffic capacities, but rush-hour traffic in I2 is twice that on I1 with probabilities of congestion being \(P(E_1)=0.1\) and \(P(E_2)=0.2\).

  • When I2 has excessive traffic, the chance of excessive traffic on I1 increases to \(P(E_1|E_2)=0.4\).
  • If there is congestion in either I1 or I2 there will be congestion in I3
  • When there is no congestion on either I1 or I2, the probability of congestion in I3 is 0.2 assuming that I3 has the same capacity as I1 and I2. What is the probability of congestion in I3 on any given day?

Problem 6.2

Successful completion (\(C\)) of a project depends on supply of materials, labor, and weather:

  • Good weather (\(G\)) and at least labor (\(L\)) or material (\(M\)) available
  • Bad weather but both labor and material available

Suppose \(P(L)=0.7\), \(P(G)=0.6\), and \(L\) is independent of both \(M\) and \(G\).

  • If the weather is good, adequate supply of materials is certain.
  • If weather is bad, probability of materials is 50%.

Describe the event of successful completion in terms of \(G\), \(L\), and \(M\).

Determine the probability of successful completion.

If the project completes successfully, what is the probability that labor supply had been inadequate?
