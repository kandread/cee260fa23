Probability

Quantifies the likelihood that an outcome of a random experiment will occur

\[P(A) = \dfrac{n}{N} = \dfrac{\text{# outcomes in A}}{\text{# outcomes in sample space}}\]

Equally likely outcomes

Whenever a sample space consists of \(N\) possible outcomes that are equally likely, the probability of each outcome is \(1/N\)

Example

Sample space: \(\{a, b, c, d\}\)

\[a=0.1, b=0.3, c=0.5, d=0.1\] A={a,b}; B={b,c,d}; C={d}

  • \(P(A)\)
  • \(P(B)\)
  • \(P(C^c)\)
  • \(P(A \cup B)\)
  • \(P(A \cap C)\)
  • \(0.1+0.3 = 0.4\)
  • \(0.3+0.5+0.1 = 0.9\)
  • \(1.0 - 0.1 = 0.9\)
  • \(0.1+0.3+0.5+0.1=1\)
  • \(0\)

Conditional probability

When prior information is available

  • Roll a pair of dice
  • 36 possible outcomes with 1/36 probability
  • First die lands on 3, what is the probability that the sum of the dice is 8?
  • Only 6 possible outcomes: (3, 1..6)
  • Each of these now has probability 1/6
  • Only one of these can result in a sum of 8

Conditional probability of an event A given event B (\(P(B)>0\)) is denoted as \(P(A|B)\) and calculated from \[P(A|B) = \dfrac{P(AB)}{P(B)}\]

https://setosa.io/conditional

Statistical independence

If two events \(A\) and \(B\) are statistically independent then \[P(A|B) = P(A)\] \[P(B|A) = P(B)\]

\[P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B) = P(B|A)P(A)\] \[\Rightarrow P(A \cap B) = P(A) P(B)\]
