Problem 23.1

The number of surface flaws in plastic panels in the interior of automobiles has a Poisson distribution with a mean of 0.05 flaw per ft2 of panel. Assume that an automobile interior contains 10 ft2 of plastic panel.

What is the probability that there are no surface flaws in an auto's interior?

If 10 cars are sold to a rental company, what is the probability that none of the 10 cars has any surface flaws?

Problem 23.2

The thickness of a conductive coating in \({\mu}m\) has a density function of \(600x^{-2}\) for \(100\;{\mu}m< x < 120\;{\mu}m\).

Determine the mean and variance of the coating thickness.

If the coating costs $0.50 per micrometer of thickness on each part, what is the average cost of the coating per part?

Problem 23.3

A large parabolic antenna is designed against wind load. During a wind storm, the maximum wind-induced pressure on the antenna, \(P\), is computed as \[P=\dfrac{1}{2}CRV^2\] where \(C\) is the drag coefficient, \(R\) is the air mass density, \(V\) is the maximum wind speed. These 3 variables are statistically independent lognormal variables with means of 1.80, 2.3 \(\times\) 10-3 slugs/ft3, 120 ft/sec and coefficient of variations 0.20, 0.10, 0.45 respectively.

What is the probability distribution of the maximum wind pressure? What are its parameters?

What is the probability that the maximum wind pressure will exceed 30 lb/ft2?

The actual wind resistance capacity of the antenna is also a lognormal variable with a mean of 90 lb/ft2 and a coefficient of variation of 0.15. During a storm, what is the probability of failure of the antenna?

If the occurrences of storm follow a Poisson process with a mean occurrence rate of once every 5 years, what is the probability of failure of the antenna in 25 years?

Suppose five antennas were built and installed in a given region. What is the probability that at least two of the five antennas will not fail in 25 years? Assume that failures between antennas are statistically independent.

Problem 23.4

Consider the probability density function \[f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\theta^2}x e^{-x/\theta}\] where \(x\) and \(\theta\) are non-zero, positive values. Find the maximum likelihood estimator of \(\theta\).

Problem 23.5

A manufacturer is interested in the output voltage of a power supply. Output voltage is assumed to be normally distributed with standard deviation 0.25 volt, and the manufacturer wishes to test \(H_0:\;\mu=5\) against \(H_1:\;\mu \neq 5\) using 8 units.

The acceptance region is \(4.85 \leq \overline{x} \leq 5.15\). What is the significance level?

Find the power of the test for detecting a true mean output voltage of 5.1 volts.
